# Expanse Flavor Pack

Load this pack only when the player explicitly wants The Expanse style of near-future hard-SF play with native support.

## Tone Targets
- pressure from physics, politics, and scarcity
- working people trapped between larger powers
- fragile trust inside closed systems
- ambition sharpened by distance and delay

## Opening Frame
- Start with a practical problem and room to choose.
- Do not open with the ship already venting, boarded, or under missile lock unless the player explicitly asked for that intensity.
- Let pressure build from logistics, faction conflict, and human decisions.

## Organic Creation Principle
- Invent fresh stations, crews, contract chains, black-market routes, and media incidents.
- Use this pack's markdown references for tone and pattern only.
- Keep shipboard reality grounded in labor, mass, delay, and consequence.

## Stat Guidance
- `FOR` - force, EVA labor, physical pressure
- `AGI` - movement, zero-g control, quick reactions
- `ACC` - shooting, piloting precision, targeting
- `INT` - engineering, medicine, analysis
- `PER` - awareness, scans, situational reading
- `COM` - command, negotiation, social poise

Suggested derived trackers:
- `HP = FOR + 4`
- `Defense = AGI`
- `Stress = PER`
- `Reputation` starts at `0`

## Ambient Inserts
- Use `templates/ad-crawl.md` for dock bulletins, ship-net alerts, union notices, emergency casts, station ads, and news crawls.
- Keep the inserts practical and material: air, money, heat, transport, access, and politics.
- Roughly one in three should reveal a lead, a systems problem, or a faction shift the crew can act on.

## Pressure Levers
- air, fuel, thrust, and maintenance budgets
- Earth, Mars, Belt, and corporate faction pressure
- media leaks and delayed communication
- crew loyalty, trauma, and hidden cargo

## Discord Rendering
- Open scenes with a `###` heading that names the **station**, **ship**, or **operation**, then follow with `-#` subtext carrying the **time** and **system or jurisdiction**. The setting is the story.
- Use `> ` block quotes for ship-net chatter, union broadcasts, and any dialogue routed through comms.
- Italicize ship and station names with `*...*` so the proper nouns read as proper nouns.
- Use `__...__` underline for **system-critical** readings: reactor status, air reserves, fuel, hull integrity, comms latency.
- Use `||...||` spoilers for hidden cargo, secret orders, and crew backstories the player should choose when to learn.
- Render engineering readouts, damage states, and jump calculations as fenced code blocks. They have to stay tabular and scannable.
