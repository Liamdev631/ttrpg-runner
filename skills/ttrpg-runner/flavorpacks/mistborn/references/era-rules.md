# Mistborn Era Rules

Pick the era before you build characters or frame the opening scene. Era choice is a hard boundary for this pack.

## Session Rule

- If the player says `mistborn` without an era, ask `Era 1` or `Era 2`.
- Record the answer in `session.json` as `mistborn_era`.
- Keep every character sheet, faction, item list, and NPC build aligned to that era.
- If the table wants a crossover, alt-history, or timeskip, confirm that explicitly instead of blending assumptions by accident.

## Era 1

Use `Era 1` for Final Empire and immediate-collapse play.

- The imported `Base Game` folder is the mechanical backbone.
- The imported `Nobles - The Golden Mandate`, `Skaa - Tin & Ash`, and `Terris - Wrought of Copper` folders are valid live setting material.
- Character options may follow the imported `Character Creation.md` assumptions: no powers, Misting, Mistborn, Feruchemist, or kandra mimicry when the table wants those options.
- Great houses, obligators, the Steel Ministry, skaa oppression, ashfall, and preindustrial logistics are live current-era assumptions unless the table says otherwise.

## Era 2

Use `Era 2` for industrial Scadrial: rail lines, newspapers, city politics, constables, syndicates, firearms, and modern commerce.

- A character may have at most one Allomantic power.
- A character may have at most one Feruchemical power.
- A Twinborn is allowed because it is one Allomantic power plus one Feruchemical power.
- Do not offer Mistborn or full Feruchemists as standard player-character options.
- Treat the imported `Nobles - The Golden Mandate`, `Skaa - Tin & Ash`, and `Terris - Wrought of Copper` folders as historical context unless the table is explicitly exploring legacy fallout from them.
- Use the imported `Base Game` mechanics only where they do not contradict the Era 2 restrictions above.

## Safe Reuse Across Eras

These imported docs remain broadly useful in both eras:

- `Base Game/Character Sheet Definitions.md`
- `Base Game/Rolling the Dice.md`
- `Base Game/Contests.md`
- `Base Game/Conflicts.md`
- `Base Game/Damage.md`
- `Base Game/Burdens.md`
- `Base Game/Complications.md`
- `Base Game/Nudges.md`

These imported docs are usually era-sensitive and should be handled with care:

- `Base Game/Character Creation.md`
- `Base Game/Equipment.md`
- `Base Game/Magic/*.md`
- everything in `Nobles - The Golden Mandate/`
- everything in `Skaa - Tin & Ash/`
- everything in `Terris - Wrought of Copper/`

When an imported rule implies broader power access than Era 2 allows, the Era 2 restrictions in this file win.
