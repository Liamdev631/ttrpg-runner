# Mistborn Era 1

Use this file with `PACK.md` for games set during the Final Empire and the original trilogy.

This file only covers things that are specific to Era 1.

# Campaign Frame

Era 1 is oppressive, secretive, and unstable.

Use these as the default pillars of play:

- the Final Empire is still standing
- the Lord Ruler, the Steel Ministry, and noble houses dominate public life
- skaa labor, fear, and rebellion are central social facts
- Terris people are controlled, monitored, and exploited
- ash, mist, shortages, and informants create constant pressure

Good Era 1 campaign types:

- skaa rebellion crew
- noble intrigue and house war
- criminal crew under imperial pressure
- secret Keeper or Terris rescue game
- hunt for forbidden knowledge or hidden metals

# Era 1 Character Assumptions

## Skaa

Skaa are a real social class in Era 1 and should matter in play.

Use these defaults:

- full skaa are normally non-Allomantic
- half-noble skaa may be Mistings or Mistborn
- skaa-specific stunts, networks, and survival traits belong here, not in Era 2
- being skaa should affect risk, access, and how authorities react

Do not carry the skaa caste forward into Era 2.

## Nobles

Nobility is a legal and political order, not just family history.

Use these defaults:

- noble blood still grants real access and protection
- noble family politics are a front-line source of missions and enemies
- arranged marriages, patronage, scandals, and house rivalries are common pressure points

The repo's noble-family mechanics fit here best.

## Terris

Terris people are distinct in both eras, but Era 1 adds special pressure:

- Terris breeding and control are active parts of the setting
- stewards, Keepers, and hidden Feruchemists are especially meaningful
- open Terris resistance is dangerous and secretive

## Kandra

Kandra exist in Era 1, but should stay rare and secret-heavy.

Use them as:

- contracted infiltrators
- ancient keepers of information
- unsettling allies with hidden motives

# Era 1 Metallic Arts

## Allomancy

Player-facing Allomancy should usually be restricted to what the world openly knows or could plausibly discover during the campaign.

Safe default:

- allow the commonly known Final Empire metals first
- treat secret metals as discoveries, rewards, or villain advantages

Ban or heavily restrict these for normal starting play:

- duralumin
- chromium
- nicrosil
- cadmium
- bendalloy
- electrum
- aluminum, unless the campaign is explicitly about hidden metallurgical secrets
- lerasium and other god metals

Also keep these tightly controlled:

- atium
- malatium
- any metal whose existence would radically change the campaign's power scale

If the table wants "standard" Era 1, players should not casually start with late-discovery metals.

## Feruchemy

Era 1 supports:

- full Feruchemists
- Keepers
- Terris metalminds

Do not assume Era 2 options exist here:

- no Ferrings as a normal option
- no Twinborn as a normal option

## Hemalurgy

Hemalurgy is a horror element in Era 1.

Default use:

- Steel Inquisitors
- koloss
- kandra blessings
- terrifying experiments and hidden plots

Keep it mostly GM-facing unless the campaign is specifically about moral collapse.

# Era 1 Setting Facts That Do Not Carry Forward

These are specific to Era 1 and should not be treated as evergreen:

- skaa exist as a legally oppressed underclass
- the Steel Ministry is a central institution
- obligators and inquisitors are major public threats
- plantations and ash-covered imperial logistics shape daily life
- noble houses operate under the Final Empire's rules

After the end of Era 1, the world changes enough that these assumptions stop being baseline.

# Era 1 Technology And Gear

Keep the tech level grounded in pre-industrial fantasy.

Normal equipment:

- blades, clubs, bows, shields, armor
- horses, carriages, barges
- lockpicks, disguises, medical supplies, rope
- mistcloaks, vials, clips, and metalminds

Do not treat these as normal Era 1 equipment:

- firearms
- rail travel
- electric technology
- modern industrial countermeasures

# Era 1 GM Pressure

If you need quick complications, reach for:

- an obligator asking questions
- a noble house covering up a scandal
- skaa workers on the edge of revolt
- an atium-related secret
- a hazekiller squad
- a kandra contract
- a koloss threat
- a hidden Keeper cell

# Era 1 Tone

The best Era 1 games feel like a collision between heist fiction, rebellion, and courtly horror.

Keep the focus on:

- fear of authority
- hidden identity
- unequal power
- dangerous secrets
- small crews making impossible moves
