# Pokemon Flavor Pack

Load this pack only when the player explicitly wants Pokemon-style play with native support.

## Tone Targets

- friendship, discovery, and competitive ambition
- bright wonder with room for danger and grief
- communities, ecosystems, and travel
- rivalry without cruelty as the default mode

## Opening Frame

- Start with curiosity and choice, not instant catastrophe.
- Let the trainer and partner establish direction before the region tests them.
- Escalate danger through player movement, rival pressure, or ecological imbalance.

## Organic Creation Principle

- Invent fresh towns, gym circuits, ranger problems, research mysteries, and team schemes.
- Use this pack's markdown references as tone support, not as a replacement for authored scenes.
- Treat partner bonds and local culture as core drivers of play.

## Stat Guidance

Character definitions (stats, derived trackers, trainer + partner sheet anchors) and the companion turn rules live in [`resources/game_definitions.md`](./resources/game_definitions.md). That file is a supplementary pack: it pairs with this `SKILL.md`, or can be loaded on its own when a non-Pokemon session wants to import Pokemon's character sheet and combat resolution.

## Gameplay Loop

1. Frame the route, town, lab, or event with a visible opportunity.
2. Tie every challenge to a relationship, habitat, or ambition.
3. Roll when a capture, race, rescue, or conflict becomes uncertain.
4. Let setbacks create detours, rival growth, or local trouble instead of dead ends.
5. Track trust, public consequences, and the state of the environment.
6. Record party bonds, captured partners, badges, and leads in the session files.

## Ambient Inserts

- Render Pokedex blurbs, trainer network notices, contest posters, radio chatter, league ads, and ranger alerts as short in-fiction prose blocks.
- Let the world feel bright and lively without turning every public message into a cutscene.
- Roughly one in three inserts should hint at a route problem, social opportunity, or rival thread the table can chase.

## Pressure Levers

- rival trainers and league expectations
- habitat disruption and local crises
- criminal teams, smuggling rings, and unethical labs
- public image, trust, and endangered partnerships

## GM Playbook

### Strong Ingredients

- a place worth caring about
- one partner bond under pressure
- a rival or authority figure with understandable goals
- a problem that can be solved by care, cleverness, or courage

### Good Mission Seeds

- stop a poaching ring without causing a route-wide stampede
- escort a nervous starter to its first public showcase
- investigate a gym challenge that keeps injuring local partners
- retrieve stolen field data before a criminal team weaponizes it
- help a shy ghost-type reclaim a theater stage that humans turned into a tourist trap
- race a flashy rival crew to calm a weather crisis before both teams get blamed for it
- protect a community garden where rare partners are evolving at the wrong speed and for the wrong reasons
- track down a missing daycare egg that keeps choosing its own rescuers and sabotaging the obvious suspects

## Discord Rendering

- Lean on `-#` subtext for **route**, **weather**, and **time of day**. The road is half the game.
- Use `> ` block quotes for trainer speech, professor dialogue, and the partner's inner voice.
- Italicize species and partner names with `*...*` so the world's terms read as the world's terms.
- Use `__...__` underline for **rare** creatures, **legendary** items, and **signature** moves that should feel elevated.
- Use `||...||` spoilers for evolutions, hidden identities, and crisis reveals the table should choose when to uncover.
- Render party sheets, move lists, and PC rosters as fenced code blocks so the data does not wrap on mobile.

## Source Manifest

This pack does not ship reusable story seed data.

That is intentional: the agent should author fresh towns, bonds, rivals, journeys, factions, and incidents every session instead of drawing from a bundled library that can repeat.

This pack also does not ship any downloaded source dump.

If curated external references are ever added here, convert them into markdown files and place a citations block at the top that names each source URL before any adapted notes.
