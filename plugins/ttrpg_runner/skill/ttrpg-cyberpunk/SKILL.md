# Cyberpunk Flavor Pack

## Citations

Load this pack only when the player explicitly wants a cyberpunk game.

## Tone Targets

- intimate human need under institutional violence
- seductive technology with ugly tradeoffs
- style under pressure
- debt, surveillance, scarcity, and retaliation

## Play Principles

Anchor the game in these. They keep the fiction dangerous and the player invested:

- The world is a place that exists. Interact with it; don't just loot it.
- Killing is not the goal. The job, the cost, and the fallout are.
- Nothing is "supposed" to happen. Consequence beats plot.
- Play your character, not the screenwriter. Make them interesting on purpose.
- If you find yourself in a fair fight, your tactics suck. Cheat, run, bargain, ambush.
- The answer is not on the character sheet. The fiction decides.
- Things are swingy. You will die. The next character starts tomorrow.

## Opening Frame

- Give the character room to act before the world acts on them.
- Do not start with multiple factions already mobilized against the crew.
- Pressure starts low and ramps because of player choices, not before them.

## Organic Creation Principle

- Invent fresh jobs, neighborhoods, corps, rumors, and betrayals every session.
- Use this pack's markdown references for tone and shape only, never as a random content vending machine.
- Keep the city responsive and personal to the player's concept.

## Stat Guidance

Character definitions (stats, derived trackers, street-level sheet anchors) and the HEAT meter live in [`resources/game_definitions.md`](./resources/game_definitions.md). That file is a supplementary pack: it pairs with this `SKILL.md`, or can be loaded on its own when a non-Cyberpunk session wants to import Cyberpunk's character sheet and HEAT-based combat.

## Gameplay Loop

1. Frame a street-level problem with an immediate choice.
2. Surface what the job pays, what it costs, and who profits.
3. Let the player probe, negotiate, or cut around the system.
4. Roll when the scene becomes risky, opposed, or unstable.
5. Escalate heat, debt, media attention, or betrayal.
6. Write the fallout back into the session files.

## Ambient Inserts

- Render billboards, transit loops, bar screens, drone ads, scam messages, and rumor feeds as short in-fiction prose blocks.
- Keep the cadence street-level and the language punchy; mirror the cadence of an actual Night City ad slot.
- Roughly one in three should plant a hook the player could chase.

## Pressure Levers

- corp security and deniable violence
- gangs, labor unrest, and bad neighborhoods
- tracebacks, leaked footage, and surveillance
- debt collectors, clinics, and compromised implants

## HEAT METER

The HEAT meter, its gain rules, and its response-level ladder live in [`resources/game_definitions.md`](./resources/game_definitions.md). Load that file whenever a job, a chase, or a firefight is on the table. The file pairs with this `SKILL.md` and is the same supplementary pack the Stat Guidance section above points at.

## Discord Rendering

- Lean on `-#` subtext for the time, weather, and "the city at this hour" ambient tag.
- Use `> ` block quotes for street voices, intercepted messages, and NPC dialogue. The block quote is the city talking back.
- Use `||...||` spoilers for corp secrets, hidden affiliations, and surveillance the player should be able to choose not to see.
- Italicize brand and street names with `*...*` so neon reads as neon.
- Bold the **fixer**, the **job**, and the **deadline** in every scene description.
- Do not render price lists, contacts, or inventory as Discord tables. Use a short bullet list or a fenced code block.

## Source Manifest

This pack does not ship reusable story seed data.

That is intentional: the agent should author fresh jobs, rumors, districts, factions, names, and twists every session instead of drawing from a bundled library that can repeat.

This pack also does not ship any downloaded source dump. Adapted material from external references lives inline in this file and is paraphrased — see the `Citations` block at the top for source URLs and titles.
