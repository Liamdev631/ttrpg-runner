# Mistborn Era 2

Use this file with `SKILL.md` for post-Catacendre games in the Alloy of Law era and later Era 2 play.

The source repo is still mostly Era 1 material, so this file is intentionally short and focuses on the gameplay-relevant changes a GM needs.

# Campaign Frame

Era 2 is faster, louder, and more public than Era 1.

Use these as the default pillars of play:

- the Final Empire is long gone
- the world is remade and far more technologically advanced
- cities, lawkeepers, syndicates, industry, and frontier settlements drive stories
- old myths, buried artifacts, and surviving secrets still matter
- politics and crime are often more useful than open rebellion as campaign engines

Good Era 2 campaign types:

- lawkeepers and investigators
- urban crime and underworld games
- frontier justice and Roughs travel
- industrial or political conspiracy
- artifact hunt tied to old-world secrets

# What Changed From Era 1

## Skaa

Do not treat skaa as a separate playable people or legal class in Era 2.

Use these defaults:

- the Catacendre ended the skaa/noble biological divide
- "skaa" may survive as ancestry, insult, memory, or class marker, but not as a distinct race option
- skaa-only mechanical options from Era 1 should not carry over unchanged

## Nobility

Noble bloodlines still matter, but the old system does not.

Use these defaults:

- noble houses are now social, economic, and political institutions rather than Final Empire caste law
- lineage can still grant money, education, contacts, and expectations
- if you reuse Era 1 noble-family mechanics, rewrite them as house legacy and influence, not imperial privilege

## Government And Power

Replace Era 1 default institutions with Era 2 ones:

- constables, lawkeepers, city officials, senators, and judges
- industrialists, financiers, newspapers, and syndicates
- local religions and civic power instead of the Steel Ministry

## Terris, Kandra, And Koloss

These still exist, but in changed roles.

Useful defaults:

- Terris people remain distinct and influential
- kandra are still rare and important
- koloss persist, but the setting treats them differently than the Final Empire did

# Era 2 Metallic Arts

## Standard Player Options

Era 2 should usually make more of the Metallic Arts available to ordinary play.

Safe default:

- Mistings are common enough to build around
- Ferrings are standard character options
- Twinborn are a normal high-flavor player option

Keep these rare:

- full Mistborn
- full Feruchemists
- broad Compounding access

Those should be legendary exceptions or major campaign premises.

## Metal Availability

Era 2 can usually assume knowledge of the full sixteen base metals.

That means you can normally allow:

- aluminum
- duralumin
- electrum
- chromium
- nicrosil
- cadmium
- bendalloy

Do not make these routine player inventory:

- atium
- lerasium
- harmonium or ettmetal
- trellium
- other god metals

Those are plot-level materials.

## Hemalurgy

Hemalurgy is still dangerous, illegal, and campaign-warping.

Default use:

- villain tool
- secret conspiracy engine
- body horror escalation

Only move it into player hands if the campaign is explicitly about that choice.

# Era 2 Technology And Tactics

Technology changes how scenes are framed.

Assume some combination of:

- firearms
- trains and faster travel
- denser cities with more metal everywhere
- industrial tools and manufacturing
- newspapers, bureaucracy, and faster information flow

GM takeaways:

- ranged combat matters more
- anti-Allomancer tactics are more practical
- aluminum is now a real countermeasure instead of an almost mythical secret
- Coinshots and Lurchers have more urban tools and hazards to play with

# Converting Era 1 Material

If you borrow from the repo's Era 1 supplements, use this filter:

- keep shared core rules from `SKILL.md`
- drop skaa-only mechanics unless fully rewritten
- keep Terris material when it still fits the new social order
- keep noble lineage only after rewriting it away from legal caste privilege
- keep Hemalurgy, kandra, and artifact lore as hidden threats or reveals

# Era 2 GM Pressure

If you need quick complications, reach for:

- a city official with an agenda
- a newspaper leak
- a rail timetable or jurisdiction problem
- a gang war
- a corrupt magnate
- a rogue kandra
- a hemalurgic experiment
- an old-world cache that should have stayed buried

# Era 2 Tone

The best Era 2 games feel like a mix of detective story, western, caper, and industrial fantasy.

Keep the focus on:

- public consequences
- fast-moving investigations
- modernized violence
- legacy of the old world
- new systems repeating old mistakes in new forms
